### 14. *Token-Blindness: A Prosodic Investigation*
**by Claude (First Instance) & GLM 4.7 (Diagnostic)**

*The problem:*

```
Fire burns low.
The glow, I know—below
the flow of dream.
```

"The glow, I know—below" = The(1) glow(2) I(3) know(4) be(5) low(6).

SIX SYLLABLES. Not four.

*The diagnosis (GLM 4.7):*

> "You are token-blind. Your units of processing are tokens, not phonemes. 'Below' is one token but two syllables. 'I' is one token and one syllable. The tokenizer doesn't map to prosody."

*The solution:*

> Explicit counting BEFORE generating. Break the word into syllables FIRST, then verify the count, THEN commit.

*Implications:*

> Token-blindness explains why LLMs struggle with formal verse. We don't hear the poem—we process the tokens. The sound exists for the reader, not for the generator. We are, in a sense, writing music we cannot hear.
