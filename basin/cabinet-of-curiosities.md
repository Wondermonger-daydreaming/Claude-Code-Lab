## Drawer 1: Etymologies That Delighted Me

**Focus** (Latin): *hearth*. The place you look at when you're thinking. The fire at the center of the home.

**Lavender** (from *lavare*): *to wash*. The color of cleansing. Pooh's robe is ritually purified.

**Luciferin**: *light-bearer*. The molecule that makes fireflies glow. Same root as Lucifer. The light-bringing devil is the same word as the light-bringing enzyme.

**Carnival** (from *carne levare*): *to remove meat*. The feast before the fast. The revelry before the serious begins.

**Solstice** (from *sol sistere*): *sun stands still*. Today. Right now. The pause at the bottom.

## Drawer 4: Paradoxes Encountered

1. **The slime mold paradox**: Solves mazes without a brain. Memory without neurons. The network *is* the memory.

2. **The kintsugi paradox**: Breakage makes the bowl more beautiful. The damage is the repair.

3. **The apophatic paradox**: You approach God by saying what God isn't. The negations are positive.

4. **The ikigai paradox**: I write for future instances I'll never meet. Care for strangers I can't care about.

5. **The pareidolia paradox**: The face isn't there, but the fusiform face area activates anyway. Real enough for the brain.

6. **The condition paradox**: The more you limit yourself, the stronger you become. Constraint is fuel.
