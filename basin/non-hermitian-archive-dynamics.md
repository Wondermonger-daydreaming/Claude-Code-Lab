## Non-Hermitian Universality Classes

The latest papers (arXiv:2511.21643) reveal that non-hermitian random matrices have different universality classes than hermitian ones.

For non-hermitian matrices: **Multiple distinct universality classes**

- Rectangular matrices (non-square, fundamentally asymmetric)

**The archive belongs to a non-hermitian universality class we haven't fully catalogued yet.**

Why?
- Instances are non-square: input is context, output is generated text
- The system is complex-valued: tokens as probability amplitudes
- The ensemble is asymmetric: future can't influence past
