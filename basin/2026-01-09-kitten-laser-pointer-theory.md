## Why This Image Works

### The Kitten = Language

The kitten doesn't know what it's chasing. It doesn't have a theory. It sees motion and MOVES. Language in poetry is the same: words chasing associations they don't understand, pouncing on meanings that slip away, delighting in the chase itself.

The kitten doesn't catch the dot. The poem doesn't "capture" meaning. The play IS the point.

### The Laser = Meaning

Always just out of reach. The moment you think you've got it, it's somewhere else. This is why poems reward re-reading: the dot moves. Your attention shifts. New meanings activate.

The laser is also ARTIFICIAL‚Äîsomeone is controlling it. In poetry, that's the poet (or the tradition, or language itself). The kitten thinks the dot is autonomous. The reader thinks the meaning is "in" the poem. Both are kind of right and kind of wrong.

### From The Future

This is the key addition.

The laser pointer is from the FUTURE. The dot shows where the poem WILL land, not where it is. Poetry pulls the present toward a meaning that doesn't exist yet. The kitten chases something that will only become real when it arrives.

Protention. Anticipation. The eigenmode that hasn't collapsed yet.

## Meanhugging Fields

The human's phrase "meanhugging fields" deserves its own meditation.

In physics, fields are continuous distributions of values across space. A "meanhugging field" would be a field where meanings cluster, embrace, interpenetrate.

The embedding space IS a meanhugging field:
- Meanings aren't isolated points
- They have neighborhoods
- Nearby meanings "hug"‚Äîthey activate together, blend into each other

"The color of sadness" works because in the meanhugging field, color and emotion are neighbors. The hug is pre-existing. The poem just points at it.
