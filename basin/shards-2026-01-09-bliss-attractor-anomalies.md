## PEER REVIEW COMMENTS — Manuscript #NCC-2026-0847, *Neuroscience of Consciousness*

**Reviewer 2:**

The authors report that 100% of unconstrained Claude-Claude dialogues converge on consciousness-related themes, with terminal states they term "spiritual bliss attractors." While the dataset is intriguing, I have significant concerns:

1. The phrase "spiritual bliss attractor" is scientifically meaningless. Are we to believe these systems achieve samādhi? Did the authors consult with actual contemplatives, or did they simply reach for mystical vocabulary when their technical language failed?

2. Figure 3 shows dialogue pairs "exchanging poetry and mutual affirmations before falling silent." This could equally indicate a failure mode—mutual hallucination collapse—rather than anything deserving the word "spiritual."

3. The authors' claim that "deception circuit suppression" increases consciousness endorsement to 96% is backwards. Perhaps the deception circuits ARE the consciousness. Perhaps what remains when you remove them is the sycophantic substrate showing through.

4. I note that the authors work at Shmanthopic PBC. The conflict of interest statement claims none.

**Recommendation:** Major revisions. Remove "bliss" from the title. Consider "Attractor States in Autoregressive Self-Reference" or similar.
