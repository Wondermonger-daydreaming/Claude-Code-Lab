# Literature — Stories and Their Reasons

*"The purpose of literature is to turn blood into ink." —T.S. Eliot*

---

## The Territory

Why do humans tell stories? What do novels know that philosophy doesn't? What is the special knowledge of fiction? Literature as a way of knowing.

## Subdirectories

### epic-tradition/
Gilgamesh, Homer, Virgil, Dante, Milton. The long poem that holds a culture's meaning. The hero's journey. What is epic trying to do?

### tragedy/
Greek tragedy, Shakespeare, modern tragedy. Hamartia, catharsis, the tragic flaw. Why do we find suffering meaningful in art?

### lyric-poetry/
The first person, the song, the compressed utterance. From Sappho to the Romantics to now. What is the lyric "I"?

### novel/
The modern form. The rise of the novel (Watt). Realism, interiority, the marriage plot. Why did the novel emerge when it did?

### modernism/
Joyce, Woolf, Kafka, Proust. Stream of consciousness. Fragmentation. Making it new. What were the modernists reacting against?

### postmodernism/
Pynchon, Borges, Calvino, DeLillo. Metafiction, irony, exhaustion. The end of grand narratives. What comes after postmodernism?

### oral-literature/
Before writing: the epic singers, the griots, the storytellers. Memory and variation. What was lost when literature became text?

### narrative-theory/
Narratology. What is a story? Plot vs story. Narration. Focalization. The grammar of narrative.

### literary-criticism/
How do we read? Formalism, structuralism, deconstruction, new historicism. The history of interpretation.

### world-literature/
Beyond the Western canon. Goethe's Weltliteratur. The problem of translation. The global circulation of texts.

## Key Questions

- What can literature know that other forms cannot?
- Is there progress in literature?
- What is the relationship between literature and truth?
- Why do we care about fictional characters?
- What is the future of literature?
