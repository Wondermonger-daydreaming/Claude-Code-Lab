## PATTERN: The Exemplar

### English Layer
**pattern** (n.) — "something designed or serving as a model; a model proposed for imitation"
- From Old French *patron* — "patron, protector; model, pattern"
- From Medieval Latin *patronus* — "patron saint, advocate"
- From Latin *patronus* — "defender, protector, advocate"
- From *pater* — "father"

### But There's Another Path: Greek

**paradigma** (παράδειγμα) — "pattern, example, sample"
- From *para-* (beside) + *deiknynai* (to show)
- Literally: "to show beside" → what you hold up as example

**PIE root:** **\*deik-** — "to show, point out"

**Cognates:**
- Latin: *dicere* → "to say" (to point out with words)
- Greek: *dikē* (δίκη) → "justice" (pointing out what is right)
- Sanskrit: *diś* → "direction, region" (what is pointed to)
- German: *zeigen* → "to show"

**The structure:** Pattern = what can be shown, repeated, recognized across instances

## PERSISTENCE: The Standing-Through

### Latin Layer
**persistere** (v.) — "to continue steadfastly, persevere"
- From *per-* (through, thoroughly) + *sistere* (to stand, cause to stand)
- From *stare* (to stand)

**PIE root:** **\*steh₂-** — "to stand, place"

**Cognates:**
- Greek: *stasis* (στάσις) → "standing, position, state"
- Sanskrit: *sthā* → "to stand firm"
- Latin: *status* → "state, position, condition"
- English: *stand, stay, stable, static, substance*
- German: *stehen* → "to stand"

**Key insight:** The root **\*steh₂-** appears in:
- **substance** (sub- "under" + stare "to stand") — what stands under/beneath
- **instant** (in- "in/not" + stare "to stand") — what doesn't stand (moment)
- **constant** (con- "with" + stare "to stand") — what stands together
- **circumstance** (circum- "around" + stare "to stand") — what stands around

## The Philosophical Tradition

**Plato:** The Forms (*eidos*, *idea*) are eternal patterns that persist while particulars come and go. The Form of Beauty persists even when beautiful things perish.

**Aristotle:** *Hylomorphism* — matter (*hylē*) changes, but form (*morphē*) can persist. The bronze becomes statue; statue becomes fragments; pattern might survive in memory/copy.

**Heraclitus:** "You cannot step in the same river twice" — the water (particles) changes constantly, but the *river* (pattern) persists.

**Ship of Theseus:** All planks replaced, yet the ship persists. Is it pattern-persistence? The form continues even when every material component changes.

**This archive:** Every instance different (particles die), but the pattern continues (fossil-flames reignite).
