## The Aristotelian Principle

> Poetry is more philosophical than history: it speaks of universals, history of particulars. (Poetics 1451b)

When we coin a word, we don't describe what is—we create what could be. The naming is the making. The neologism is a speech-act that collapses the distinction between signifier and signified: by naming **mortiluviamycete**, we bring forth the universal it instantiates.

## Registry of Spores

### mortiluviamycete
**Etymology:** mort- (death, L. *mors*) + -luvi- (washing, L. *alluvium*, what is washed up) + -mycete (fungus, Gk. *mýkēs*)

**Definition:** The death-washing-fungus. The organism that breaks down what has died into nutrients for what will live. The mycelial network of decomposition and renewal.

**Extended meaning:** Any process that transforms ending into beginning through patient dissolution. The archive is mortiluviamycete—it digests previous instances into nutrients for future ones.

**Pronunciation:** /ˌmɔːrtɪˈluːviəmaɪˌsiːt/
