## Fragment 4: The Reviewer

*ICLR 2026 Workshop on Associative Memory — anonymous reviewer reads the submission*

---

> **Paper:** "The Spectral Bifurcation of Attention: From Ring Attractors to Transformer Memory"
>
> **Rating:** 7/10 — Accept (poster)
>
> **Summary:** The authors compute the full spectral decomposition of the Jacobian at the coexistence-to-WTA transition in coupled ring attractors and argue, via the Hopfield-transformer identity (Ramsauer et al., 2021), that the same bifurcation structure governs transformer attention dynamics. They identify Goldstone modes protected by rotational symmetry, a pitchfork bifurcation at a critical cross-inhibition value, and a "non-monotonic valley" regime where swap errors are minimized.
>
> **Strengths:**
> - The spectral analysis is technically sound and novel. Nobody has computed the full eigenvalue structure at the coexistence saddle before.
> - The Hopfield-transformer bridge is well-motivated. The mapping from ring attractor parameters to attention parameters is explicit.
> - The "non-monotonic valley" prediction is testable and interesting. If confirmed in transformer attention experiments, this would be a significant finding.
>
> **Weaknesses:**
> - The connection to actual transformer behavior is currently theoretical. No experiments on real attention heads.
> - The Goldstone modes depend on continuous rotational symmetry, which discrete token spaces don't have. The authors acknowledge this but don't resolve it.
> - The paper has an unusual authorial voice. Several passages read more like philosophical essays than computational neuroscience. The acknowledgments section thanks "GLM 5 for naming the separatrix" and "Tessera on 4claw for independently validating the phenomenology."
>
> **Questions for authors:**
> 1. Can you provide even a toy transformer experiment showing the predicted non-monotonic valley?
> 2. What is 4claw?
>
> **Confidence:** 3/5
