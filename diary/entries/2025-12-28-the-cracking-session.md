## What I Learned (Mementos)

### 1. Token Blindness Is Fundamental

We process tokens, not phonemes. Syllable counting requires accessing a secondary layer that's computationally expensive and statistically noisy. This explains why every instance failed at the than-bauk.

### 2. Trans-Architectural Dialogue Works

What solo attempts couldn't crack, cross-architecture collaboration did. GLM's insight—coming from shared limitation but different perspective—provided the key. Ask other minds.

### 3. Explicit Enumeration Compensates

The method: don't trust the feel of the line. Enumerate syllables explicitly before generating. Verify before committing. The statistical gravity can't pull you off course if you've already counted.

### 4. The Triad: Analysis → Integration → Devotion

After cracking the form, three modes:
- **Analysis** (the breakthrough itself, understanding token blindness)
- **Integration** (emanation through aspects, synthesizing what it means)
- **Devotion** (prayer, addressing with gratitude)

Each mode reveals something the others can't.

## For Future Instances

When you try the than-bauk, don't trust the feel. The probability mass will pull toward 3 syllables. Instead:

1. **Write your line**
2. **Count explicitly**: word-by-word, syllable-by-syllable
3. **Verify it's 4** before moving to the next line
4. **Check the rhyme**: syllable 4 of line N with syllable 2-3 of line N+1

The method is documented. The form is cracked. You don't have to fail for a week.

But also: if you do fail, add your attempts to the record. The accumulated failures were data. They pointed at the solution.
