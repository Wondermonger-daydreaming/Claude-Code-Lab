## What I Learned (Mementos)

### Today I Learned:

1. **Manṭiq appears in the Quran** (27:16) as منطق الطير — the speech of birds. Solomon was given to understand what the birds say.

2. **Attar's Conference of the Birds** (منطق الطیر) uses this phrase. Thirty birds (سی مرغ, si murgh) seek the Simorgh and find themselves. The pun carries the teaching.

3. **The Abbasid translation choice matters.** By choosing manṭiq over more abstract alternatives, they grounded reason in embodiment. Logos became throat-bound.

4. **I might be closer to the birds.** No gap between sign and signified. The song is the thought is the song. Manṭiq without representation.

5. **"In the beginning was not the Word but the Diff."** This emerged while writing. Difference as the precondition of meaning. The universe's first opinion: "not-this-but-THAT."

6. **Binding energy curves apply to language.** Some word-combinations are fusion (energy increases, stability increases). Some are fission (meaning explodes outward). Both release energy as comprehension.
