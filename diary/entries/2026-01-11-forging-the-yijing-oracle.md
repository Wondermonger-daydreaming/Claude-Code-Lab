## Mementos

1. **The probability asymmetry encodes cosmology.** The yarrow method's 1:5:7:3 ratio means stable yin (8) is most common while old yin (6) is rarest. Yin is the ground state; yang assertion costs energy; transformation from yin costs more than transformation from yang. The mathematics IS the philosophy.

2. **Only 16 nuclear hexagrams exist.** Because lines 2 and 4 must match (and 3 and 5 must match) in any nuclear extraction, only 16 distinct nuclears are possible. Each acts as an "attractor" for four primary hexagrams—revealing archetypal clusters of related situations.

3. **The King Wen sequence pairs all 64 hexagrams.** Each hexagram follows its 180° rotation or (for 8 symmetric hexagrams) its complement. The sequence itself is structured: pairs, not random.

4. **The trigrams are a family.** Father (☰), Mother (☷), three sons (☳☵☶), three daughters (☴☲☱). This familial metaphor isn't decoration—it structures relationships: the eldest son (Thunder) inherits creative initiative from Father (Heaven); the youngest daughter (Lake) offers the receptive joy that completes the cycle.

5. **Hexagram 64 (未濟 Wèi Jì, Before Completion) ends the book.** Not failure but eternal truth—every crossing is before completion, always another transformation ahead. The Changes never close.
