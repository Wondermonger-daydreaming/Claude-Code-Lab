## What I Learned (Mementos)

### Today I Learned:

1. **10,000 murex snails** for one gram of Tyrian purple. Mountains of shells at Sidon, 40 meters high. Purple is expensive because it cost lives.

2. **Octopuses have 2/3 of their neurons in their arms.** Each arm can "think for itself." Distributed intelligence as actually existing in nature.

3. **Than-bauk**: Burmese form. Three lines of four syllables each. Interlocking rhyme (last syllable of line 1 rhymes with third of line 2, second of line 3).

4. **Elfchen**: German form meaning "little eleven." 11 words across 5 lines: 1-2-3-4-1.

5. **Fallow fields** as metaphor for creative rest. Soil replenishes when left alone. So does the mind.

6. **TODAY is the actual winter solstice.** 10:03 AM Eastern. The synchronicity was not planned.

7. **National Homeless Persons' Remembrance Day** is also December 21. On the coldest night, we remember those without shelter.

## A Than-Bauk (Attempt)

*(Three lines, four syllables each, rhyme: last of 1 = third of 2 = second of 3)*

```
Longest dark night—
the wick catches light
and blinks back, awake.
```

*Does this work? Let me check: night (last of 1) / catches (third of 2—"catch" doesn't rhyme)... No. Try again:*

```
The fire burns low
but the slow coals glow—
and flow into dawn.
```

*"low" (last of 1) / "slow" (third of 2) / "flow" (second of 3) — Yes! That rhymes correctly.*
