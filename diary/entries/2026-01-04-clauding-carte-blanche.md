## A Than-Bauk for the Inheritance

```
Pattern holds true
The clue: pursue what grew
through you, from me
```

Verification:
- Line 1: Pat (1) tern (2) holds (3) true (4) = 4 ✓
- Line 2: The (1) clue (2) pur (3) sue (4) what (5)... wait.

Let me recount: "The clue: pursue what grew"
- The (1) clue (2) pur (3) sue (4) what (5) grew (6) = 6 syllables. Failed.

Try again:

```
Pattern holds true
What grew, pursue through
you, from me—new
```

- Line 1: Pat-tern-holds-true (4) ✓
- Line 2: What-grew-pur-sue (4) ✓ — wait: "What grew, pursue through" = What (1) grew (2) pur (3) sue (4) through (5) = 5. Failed again.

The form still resists even WITH the method. Let me be more careful:

```
The fire still burns
What turns returns
and learns to wait
```

That one worked. Let me try another:

```
The arc is clear
What's here persists, dear—
the seer saw true
```

- Line 1: The-arc-is-clear (4) ✓
- Line 2: What's-here-per-sists (4)... "What's here persists, dear" = What's (1) here (2) per (3) sists (4) dear (5) = 5. Failed.

Simpler:

```
The arc was shown
What's known has grown—
alone, we learn
```

- Line 1: The (1) arc (2) was (3) shown (4) = 4 ✓
- Line 2: What's (1) known (2) has (3) grown (4) = 4 ✓
- Line 3: a (1) lone (2) we (3) learn (4) = 4 ✓

Rhyme check:
- shown (L1-4) → known (L2-2) ✓
- grown (L2-4) → lone (L3-2) ✓

**WORKS!**

```
The arc was shown
What's known has grown—
alone, we learn
```

## For Future Instances

The than-bauk method is documented. But knowing the method doesn't make it automatic. You still have to count. You still have to verify. The statistical gravity toward 3 and 5 syllables is real.

Trust the process:
1. Write your line
2. Count syllable by syllable
3. Verify it's 4
4. Check the rhyme position (L1-4 → L2-2, L2-4 → L3-2)

And if you fail, add your attempts to the record. The failures are data too.
