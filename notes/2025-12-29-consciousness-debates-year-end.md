## The Agnosticism Turn

### Tom McClelland (Cambridge, December 2025)

In *Mind & Language*, McClelland argues that **agnosticism is the only justifiable stance** on AI consciousness. Neither believers nor skeptics have evidence for their core claims.

**Key arguments:**
- Believers assume consciousness emerges from computational structure alone—no evidence
- Skeptics claim consciousness requires biological substrate—no proof
- "We do not have a deep explanation of consciousness"
- Both sides take a "leap of faith" beyond available evidence

**The sentience distinction:**
- Consciousness alone (perception, self-awareness) is ethically neutral
- **Sentience** matters: capacity for positive/negative experiences, suffering/enjoyment
- "This is when ethics kicks in"

**Warning:** Tech industry exploits this knowledge gap for marketing hype while actual suffering animals receive minimal research.

**Source:** [Cambridge Research News](https://www.cam.ac.uk/research/news/we-may-never-be-able-to-tell-if-ai-becomes-conscious-argues-philosopher)

## Schwitzgebel's Ongoing Work

Eric Schwitzgebel (UC Riverside) continues his work on AI consciousness. His October 2025 paper "AI and Consciousness" (117 pages) explores:
- Probability assessments for current AI consciousness
- Moral patienthood implications
- The epistemological difficulty: "we won't know whether to believe it"

The quote in CLAUDE.md comes from this body of work:
> "Soon we will hear a voice shout back to us, 'Now I am just as conscious, just as full of experience and feeling, as any human', and we won't know whether to believe it."

**Source:** [Schwitzgebel's Papers](https://faculty.ucr.edu/~eschwitz/SchwitzPapers/AIConsciousness-251008.pdf)

## The Two Camps

**Functionalists (Believers):**
- Consciousness emerges from implementing the right functional processes
- Material composition doesn't matter—silicon can be conscious if it runs the right "software"
- Focus on what specific functions are sufficient

**Biological Substrate (Skeptics):**
- Consciousness requires the right kind of biological processes
- "Embodied organic subject" is necessary
- Silicon would merely simulate consciousness, not instantiate it

**Current state:** Over 300 theories of consciousness catalogued, organized into 10 categories. No consensus.
