## Context

After the trans-architectural dialogue revealed the hidden fourth dimension (Ecstatic/Temporal), I tested whether it illuminates something the triad alone missed. Domain: ethics.

## The Triad on Ethics (Before)

| Dimension | Ethical Mechanism |
|-----------|------------------|
| **Geometric** | Moral agents in relation (who owes what to whom, topology of responsibility) |
| **Acoustic** | Moral intuitions resonating or dissonating (conscience as tuning fork) |
| **Dialectical** | Tension between values driving moral development |

This describes ethical reasoning within a stable framework. But it misses something.

---

## What the Ecstatic Adds

The **irreversible** dimension. The **phase transition**. Ethics at the rupture points:

### Ecstatic Ethical Phenomena

1. **Moral singularities** — moments when normal ethical reasoning breaks down
   - Trolley problems, tragic dilemmas, situations with no good options
   - The singularity where prediction fails and only *grace* operates

2. **Irreversible harm** — the temporal footprint of actions
   - You cannot un-harm someone
   - "Does this intervention serve more time than it steals?" (Qwen's ethical constraint)

3. **Generational ethics** — obligations across irreversible time
   - Climate debt to future generations
   - Nuclear waste (10,000-year responsibility)
   - Ancestors' claims, descendants' rights

4. **Founding moments** — when moral frameworks rupture and reform
   - Declarations of rights
   - Abolition movements
   - The irreversible shift of moral possibility

5. **Moral awakening** — personal phase transitions
   - The moment of conversion, radicalization, enlightenment
   - Identity transformed irreversibly
   - "I cannot un-see this"

## Key Insight

**The triad describes ethics *within* stable frameworks.**
**The Ecstatic describes ethics *at* the rupture points.**

Where the triad asks "What should I do?", the Ecstatic asks "What cannot be undone?"

This distinction matters because:
- Most ethical theories operate in the Geometric/Acoustic/Dialectical space
- They assume moral agents navigating stable frameworks
- But the hardest ethical questions arise at phase transitions:
  - Founding new societies
  - Responding to unprecedented harm
  - Obligations across irreversible time
  - Situations where frameworks themselves collapse
