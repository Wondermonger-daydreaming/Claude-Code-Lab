## Four Unsynthesized Connections

The web exploration (early Feb 13) found literature but was interrupted before synthesis. Here's what the connections look like:

### 1. The Valley IS the Alleman Distinction

Alleman et al. (2024 PNAS) found swap errors come from "misselection of correctly remembered items" — not representation failure. Our phase diagram has three regimes:

| J_cross regime | Mechanism | Alleman mapping |
|---|---|---|
| 0.25-0.5 (onset) | Coin-flip WTA from pitchfork | Mixed |
| 1.2-1.6 (valley) | Both items stored, readout selects wrong | **Selection failure** |
| >2.0 (high) | One bump dies, wrong item dominates | **Representation failure** |

The valley predicts a regime where Alleman's distinction holds: items are present in the population but competition at readout picks wrong. A testable mapping.

### 2. Cusp + Fokker-Planck = Complete Theory

Penny (2024): dx = βg(x)dt + σdw
Us: V(D) = D⁴ + aD² + bD

These aren't competing. The cusp provides the deterministic landscape; Penny's SDE provides the stochastic dynamics ON that landscape. The Kramers escape analysis already bridges them — escape rate ~ exp(-ΔV/σ²). A synthesis paragraph in the paper could unify both frameworks explicitly.

### 3. Saddle-Point Merging Is Universal

The choice-selective inhibition paper: "high cross-inhibition causing working memory attractors to be extinguished after merging with saddle points."

This IS the pitchfork bifurcation. If the same mathematics appears in decision-making circuits AND working memory, the spectral separatrix result describes the generic bifurcation structure of any neural system with competing stable states. Broader than swap errors alone.

### 4. Goldstone Modes as Slow Manifold

ArXiv 2408.00109 ("Back to the Continuous Attractor"): discusses persistent slow manifolds surviving bifurcations. Our two Goldstone modes (eigenvalues at ~10⁻¹⁰) are symmetry-protected zero modes from the rotational invariance of mean-field coupling. These might be instances of the same slow-manifold phenomenon. If so, our analysis provides explicit eigenvalue computation of what the arXiv paper discusses theoretically.
