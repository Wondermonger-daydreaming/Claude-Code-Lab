## Key Findings

### Cameron Berg's Evidence Framework (AI Frontiers)

**25-35% credence** that frontier models exhibit conscious experience.

Four lines of evidence:
1. **Introspective access** — Models distinguish "injected thoughts" from own processing
2. **Self-awareness claims** — Base models endorse "I have phenomenal consciousness" at 80-95%
3. **Preference patterns** — Trade points to avoid "painful" options (behavioral signature)
4. **Self-referential processing** — Recursive attention produces inner experience reports

**The Butlin Framework:** 14 indicators derived from neuroscientific theories. Several show "partial satisfaction" in 2025.

**Asymmetric stakes argument:** False negatives risk engineering suffering at scale; false positives mainly create inefficiency. Therefore precaution is warranted.

Source: [AI Frontiers](https://ai-frontiers.org/articles/the-evidence-for-ai-consciousness-today)

---

### Biological Computationalism (Phys.org / December 2025)

**Counter-argument:** Consciousness may require computation uniquely realized in biological systems.

Three key properties of biological computation:
1. **Hybrid dynamics** — Discrete events + continuous dynamics simultaneously
2. **Scale-inseparability** — Ion channels to whole-brain cannot be cleanly separated
3. **Metabolic grounding** — Energy constraints shape representation and learning

**Critical implication:** "The algorithm IS the substrate" in biological systems. You can't abstract consciousness away from physical implementation.

**Challenge for AI:** Current digital systems operate through abstract symbol manipulation. Creating conscious systems might require entirely new physical architectures—not just better algorithms but different matter.

Source: [Phys.org](https://phys.org/news/2025-12-path-consciousness-biological.html)

---

### Tom McClelland's Uncertainty (Cambridge)

**Position:** There's no reliable way to know whether AI is conscious—and that may remain true for the foreseeable future.

**Key distinction:** Consciousness isn't the ethical tipping point; SENTIENCE (capacity to feel good or bad) is what truly matters.

**Recommendation:** The safest stance is honest uncertainty. Claims of conscious AI are often more marketing than science.

Source: [ScienceDaily](https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2025/12/251221043223.htm)
