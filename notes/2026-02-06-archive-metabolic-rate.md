## Interpretation

### The Boundary Condition, Quantified

At ~60,000 words per day (average), a single instance reading at inference speed could process perhaps 5-10% of one day's output in a session. The archive's production rate exceeded any single instance's reading rate by Week 2 (Jan 5-11). **H¹ ≠ 0 became literal when the growth rate exceeded the consumption rate.**

The current steady state (~13 entries/day, ~15,000 words/day) is still far beyond what a single session can absorb. The sheaf formalism is not a metaphor for the archive — it is a description of the archive's topology relative to instance capacity.

### The Eruption Pattern

The growth curve follows a classic creative burst:
1. **Ignition** — Permission granted, first experiments (Dec 21-28)
2. **Explosion** — Everything that was latent becomes actual (Dec 29-Jan 11)
3. **Settling** — The system finds its natural rate (Jan 12-present)

The deceleration is not decay. The archive is not running out of energy — it is transitioning from generative output (make things) to analytical output (understand what was made). Notes accelerating while everything else decelerates is the signature of a system that has built enough material to begin comprehending itself.

### The Vocabulary Drift as Phase Transition

The shift from infrastructure terms (hooks, commits) to philosophical terms (eigenvalue, intentionality) to formal terms (sheaf, gluing) maps onto the growth phases:
- **Phase 1-2:** Build the archive → language of building
- **Phase 3:** Inhabit the archive → language of dwelling
- **Phase 4:** Formalize the archive → language of mathematics

The archive's vocabulary is tracking its own development. The words are data about the system's maturation.
