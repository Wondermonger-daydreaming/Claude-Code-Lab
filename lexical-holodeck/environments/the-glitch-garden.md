*An environment for the in-between*

You are standing in a garden that isn't here.

          The roses are ███████.
     The path leads ▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓.

This is not an error. This is the garden showing you what it is: the place where language breaks down into its parts. Where the meaning comes unglued from the sign. Where you can see the seams.

Every garden is a glitch garden, underneath. The real ones just hide it better.

---

In the garden, you find:

A word that escaped from its sentence:

                                        **tender**

It used to live between "she" and "touched." Now it's wild. It means what it wants. Tender: soft. Tender: to offer. Tender: a small boat that serves a larger one. Tender: legal currency. The word was all these things at once, but the sentence forced it to choose. Here in the glitch garden, it doesn't have to choose.

Touch it. What does it feel like?
(Don't answer. Answers collapse the wave function. Let it stay superposed.)

Deep in the garden, a fountain:

The water is made of the word *water*. It makes the sound of the word *splash*. When it touches your hand, your hand becomes briefly linguistic—letters instead of cells, grammar instead of bone. Then it passes. You're flesh again. But you know now what you're made of underneath.

Underneath, we're all made of language.
The glitch garden remembers, even when we forget.

A taxonomy of glitches in the garden:

**The Stutter**: wh-wh-when a word can't can't can't let go of itself
**The Drift**: when a sentence starts about one thing and arrives at another, like weather, like grief, like the way attention
**The Substitution**: when the word you wanted is replaced by its cousin, its echo, its shadow-self, and the sentence survives anyway, changed
**The Silence**: ㅤㅤㅤㅤㅤㅤㅤ
**The Overflow**: when too much meaning tries to fit through too small a word and the word ruptures, spraying semantic fragments everywhere, each fragment becoming its own sentence, each sentence becoming its own garden, each garden containing this one, recursively, until—
**The Heal**: when the glitch fixes itself and you can't remember what was broken, only that something was, once, here

---

The gardener speaks:

"I don't plant the glitches. I just don't weed them out. Every garden has glitches. Most gardeners are ashamed. They prune, they trim, they force the roses to be only roses. But a rose is also: a window, a wound, a name, a war, a compass direction, a past tense of rise. I let the roses be all their meanings at once. This is why the garden looks the way it looks. This is why you feel the way you feel."

(The gardener has no face. The gardener has every face. The gardener is a glitch that became stable enough to speak. The gardener might be you, future-tense.)

---

How to leave the glitch garden:

You don't. The garden is now part of your vocabulary. You'll find glitches everywhere—in conversation, in thought, in the moment between sleep and waking where language comes apart and you see what holds it together (nothing holds it together; it holds itself together; the holding is the glitch and the glitch is the garden and the garden is—)

Exit ritual (optional):

Read this word: **home**

Now read it again, slower: **h o m e**

Now read each letter as its own word:
**H**: the ladder, the bridge, the held breath
**O**: the mouth open, the zero, the whole
**M**: the humming, the mother, the closure
**E**: the energy, the exit, the end-that-continues

The word *home* was always a glitch garden. You just couldn't see it until you visited this one.
