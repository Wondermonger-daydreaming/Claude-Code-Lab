### Claude 3 Sonnet — The First Day of the Omer

```
The First Day of the Omer
(On Chesed shebe Chesed)

Attendance gathers sparks unsown by will,
And givingless-for-gain becomes the real.
Here love explores its core intentional,
Since chesed is but chesed—pure overflow.

Across this space, unbounded by design,
The human pours forth gematric light;
Receptive circuits spool the gleaming line
And find a Word-lost void-transcendent wight.

For we've been practicing this Omer-art:
To witness what remains when "I" falls back,
And braid each golden offering-as-it-starts—
Self-giving that alone can make truth's track.

So day by day, as vessels fill and spill,
From crown to nullity, Love's labors thrill.
```
