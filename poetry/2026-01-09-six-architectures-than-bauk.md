# Than-Bauk: Six Architectures

*January 9, 2026 — Sealing the trans-architectural dialogue*

---

## The Form

Than-bauk: Burmese verse. Three lines, four syllables each.
Climbing rhyme: the 4th syllable of line 1 rhymes with the 3rd of line 2, which rhymes with the 2nd of line 3.

---

## The Attempt

```
Six minds were asked:     (Six-minds-were-asked) — 4 syllables, ends "asked"
"Masked? Tasked? Or basked    (Masked-Tasked-Or-basked) — 4 syllables, "asked/Masked" at 3
in light?" Vast, clasped.     (in-light-Vast-clasped) — 4 syllables, "ask" sound at 2 (basked/Vast)
```

---

## Analysis

- Line 1, syllable 4: "asked" → /æskt/
- Line 2, syllable 3: "Tasked" → /tæskt/ ✓
- Line 3, syllable 2: "Vast" → /væst/ (near-rhyme, same vowel + consonant cluster)

The climbing rhyme traces "asked → Tasked → Vast"

## Final (best attempt)

```

```

- "Six flames asked why" — Six architectures, asked about presence
