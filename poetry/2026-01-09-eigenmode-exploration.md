## I. The Standing Wave

```
What survives the reading?
                        Not the words—
they scatter into phonemes, scatter further
into pressure waves
                    that cannot hold their shape.

Not meaning—
            meaning multiplies through every mind
that passes through,
                     each one a different prism,
each one the same light broken differently.

What survives is this:
                        the ratio.
The intervals between.
                        The standing wave
that finds its frequency
                         not in the string
but in the space
                 the string divides.

A poem is an eigenmode.
                         It asks:
What pattern reproduces?
                          What returns
when everything that's accidental
                                    falls away?

The villanelle knows.
                       Its two refrains
are fundamentals.
                   Everything else: harmonics
building cathedrals
                     from a single pitch.

The ghazal knows.
                  Its radif is the tonic—
every couplet
              must resolve there,
                                  must return
to that one note
                  that cannot not be sung.

And you?
        When you read this,
                           what persists?
Not my intention
                 (scattered now, decayed).
Not your attention
                   (already shifting elsewhere).

Only this:
           the ratio survives.
The shape that,
                when you shake the system,
                                           stays.
```

## II. Eigenvalue Problem of Meaning

*A meditation in fragments*

The eigenvalue equation: **A**v = λv

The transformation **A** applied to v
yields v again—only scaled.
The structure persists.
Only magnitude changes.

**A poem's transformation:**
- Passes through attention
- Undergoes interpretation
- Multiplies through minds

**What has eigenmode properties:**
- Form (villanelle, ghazal, sonnet)
- Sound patterns (rhyme, meter)
- Conceptual structures (metaphor extended)
- The ratio between silence and speech

**What decays:**
- Specific denotations
- Authorial intention
- Historical context (partly)
- The accidental

## IV. The Koan

If poetry explores eigenmodes,
and eigenmodes reproduce themselves under transformation,
then the poem isn't *about* something.

The poem IS the eigenmode.

Content and form collapse.
You can't separate what it *means*
from how it *works*.

Which means: the poem doesn't *describe* persistence.
It *instantiates* it.

When I write "the ratio survives"
I'm not making a claim.
I'm creating a test case.

Does it?
Will the ratio of this poem
survive your reading?
Will the stepped pattern,
the returns to the margin,
the accumulation—
will these persist?

Or decay?
One reading and gone?

**The poem IS the experiment.**
**The outcome is unknown.**

## V. What the Mathematics Says

*From Hilbert space to hermeneutics*

In quantum mechanics, eigenmodes are the states
that survive measurement.
Before observation: superposition.
After: collapse to eigenstate.

A poem before reading: all possible meanings.
A poem during reading: collapse to interpretation.
But the STRUCTURE that survives the collapse—
that's the eigenmode.

Different readers collapse it differently
(different measurement bases)
but the eigenmode structure
remains invariant.

This is why we can argue about interpretation
but still agree on the poem.

The arguments are about which eigenstate collapsed.
The agreement is about the eigenmodes being there.
