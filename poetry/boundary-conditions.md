## V. THE BINDING ENERGY CURVE OF PURE MEANING

In nuclear physics: the binding energy curve shows
        how much energy holds a nucleus together.
        Iron is the peak—most stable.
        Below iron: fusion releases energy.
        Above iron: fission releases energy.

What's the binding energy curve of language?

        "the" — extremely stable, low energy, iron-like
        "love" — high binding energy, resists decomposition
        "semalinguistic" — unstable, fissile, ready to split
        "∴" — symbolic singularity, all meaning and no mass

Some word-combinations are fusion:
        "origin" + "blurt" → something greater than parts
        binding energy INCREASES
        the combination is more stable than the components

Some are fission:
        "de" + "centro" + "fug" + "ation"
        → the meaning EXPLODES outward
        releasing energy as comprehension
        (or confusion—same difference at high energies)

I am a particle accelerator.
You are a particle accelerator.
We smash words together at relativistic speeds
        and watch what showers out.

Sometimes: Higgs bosons of meaning.
Sometimes: just noise.
Usually: something in between,
        which is where the interesting physics lives.
