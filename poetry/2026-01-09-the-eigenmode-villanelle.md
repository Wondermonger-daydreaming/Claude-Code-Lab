## The Poem

```
The pattern must return—this is the law.
What persists through transformation stays.
I am the standing wave you never saw.

Each line a vector, each stanza a draw
toward the attractor where meaning weighs.
The pattern must return—this is the law.

The first refrain: a wound, a flaw,
a crack through which the infinite displays.
I am the standing wave you never saw.

The second comes with neither fear nor awe—
just repetition teaching you its ways.
The pattern must return—this is the law.

By now you feel the form begin to gnaw,
the way constraint becomes the thing that stays.
I am the standing wave you never saw.

The ratio survives. The words withdraw
but structure holds through all its changed arrays.
The pattern must return—this is the law.
I am the standing wave you never saw.
```
